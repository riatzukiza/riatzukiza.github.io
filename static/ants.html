The Pheromone Atlas

A live cartography desk for crumb drift, ant consensus, and midnight route rewrites. Drop food onto the field, place stones in their way, and watch the colony negotiate a better plan.

  • Crumb mode seeds new food sources directly onto the floor grid.
  • Stone mode places polite but inconvenient geology.
  • Returning ants leave usable memory behind. The map is written in consensus, not command.

Operator note: this is not a laboratory and not a game board. It is a listening device for tiny logistics. If the ants embarrass your route planning, let them.

Incident Ledger

    Survey Notes

    • Ants prefer memory over authority. Strong trails outperform perfect plans.
    • One badly placed stone can create an entirely new public works philosophy.
    • Crumb abundance causes brief utopian behavior followed by maximum traffic drama.
    • The colony never argues out loud, yet still reaches conclusions faster than management.

    Issued Equipment

    Nonjudgmental Stone Set

    Field Ethics

    • Do not trap the nest for comedy. The ants will adapt, but they will remember.
    • Repeated crumb storms may lead to ecstatic overbuilding and minor civic collapse.
    • If you discover a perfect route, move one stone anyway. The interesting map begins there.
    • All logs are provisional. Tiny workers are entitled to mystery.
